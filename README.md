## 🎵 Core Features 💿

ParaKit-Open_Source offers modules to handle your music files.

### Stem Splitter
The stem splitter separates a song into different instrument tracks. It isolates the drums so you can hear them clearly. This makes it easier to create accurate charts.

### Auto Audio to MIDI
This feature detects the rhythm in your audio files and creates a MIDI guide. It tracks the beat and places notes where the drums occur. 

### BPM Detection
BPM detection finds the tempo of your song. Correct timing ensures your charts match the music flow.

### MIDI Editor
The built-in editor lets you adjust notes. You can move, delete, or add notes to fix timing or improve the feel of your chart.

### Asset Finder and Manager
Keep your files organized with the asset manager. It tracks your audio clips, MIDI files, and project settings in one place.

### Sheet Music Support
Import MusicXML files to turn printed sheet music into MIDI data. This feature saves time when you already have the notes written down.

## 🎹 Hardware Support

Connect your electronic drum kit or MIDI pad to your computer using a USB cable. The application recognizes most MIDI devices automatically. Select your device in the settings menu to start recording your performance directly into the software.

## ⚙️ How to Create Your First Chart

1. Open ParaKit-Open_Source.
2. Select File and choose New Project.
3. Drag your audio file into the workspace.
4. Use the BPM detector to set the song speed.
5. Use the Stem Splitter if you need a clean drum track.
6. Use the Auto Audio to MIDI feature to generate the base notes.
7. Use the MIDI Editor to refine the note placement.
8. Export your work as a .rlrr or .chart file depending on your game requirements.

## 🛡️ Troubleshooting

If the application fails to open, verify your Windows updates. Ensure your audio drivers are current. If your MIDI device does not connect, check your cable connection and ensure the device is powered on. Restart the application if you encounter performance delays.
